# Amware Brand Guidelines

> Version 1.0 — March 2026

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Amware** is the personal brand of Alec Mingione — a software engineer, founder, on-demand CTO, AI expert, mentor, and entrepreneur based in Phoenix, Arizona. The brand represents the intersection of technical excellence and creative vision: building things that are precise, purposeful, and beautifully crafted.

Amware is the public-facing identity behind Alec's writing, open-source work, projects, and professional presence. It communicates to developers, founders, and engineering leaders who value both depth of craft and clarity of execution.

It speaks to engineers who want to grow beyond their IDE, and to founders who need a technical partner who actually ships.

## 3. Logo

### Primary Logo
The **AMWare Logo** is the primary brand mark - a crown shape with the letters A - M - W; all appearing in the shape of a crown. It appears in the top navigation across all pages of amware.dev and serves as the home/identity anchor.

### Logo Usage Rules
- Always use the logo on its own — do not place text or other graphics directly on top of it.
- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "A" in AMWare on all sides.
- Do not stretch, skew, recolor, or add effects (drop shadows, gradients, outlines) to the logo.
- The logo should only appear in its approved color variants: the primary version and a monochrome (white or black) version for use on dark or light backgrounds respectively.
- Minimum size: never render the logo smaller than 24px in height in digital contexts.

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not use the wordmark without the logomark on branded headers.
- Do not rotate the logo.
- Do not place the logo on visually busy backgrounds without a clear backing field.

## 4. Color Palette

The Amware color palette is minimal, dark-first, and high-contrast — consistent with a modern developer aesthetic that feels sophisticated and focused.

### Primary Colors

| Name         | Hex       | Usage                                      |
|--------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------|
| Void Black   | `#09090b` | Primary background (dark mode base)        |
| Off-White    | `#fafafa` | Primary text on dark backgrounds           |
| Zinc 800     | `#27272a` | Secondary surface / card backgrounds       |
| Zinc 700     | `#3f3f46` | Borders, dividers, subtle containers       |
| Zinc 400     | `#a1a1aa` | Secondary / muted text                     |

### Accent Colors

| Name         | Hex       | Usage                                      |
|--------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------|
| Teal / Cyan  | `#2dd4bf` | Interactive highlights, links, CTAs        |
| Warm White   | `#e4e4e7` | Headings and emphasized text on dark bg    |

### Usage Notes
- The brand operates primarily in **dark mode**. Light mode implementations should mirror the same contrast ratios.
- Accent colors should be used sparingly — for interactive elements, callouts, and highlights only.
- Never use the teal accent as a background for large surface areas.
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 minimum) for all text/background combinations.

## 5. Typography

Amware uses a clean, modern type system that balances readability with personality.

### Type Scale

| Role              | Style                          | Notes                              |
|-------------------|--------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Display / Hero    | Large, bold, high-contrast     | Used for hero headings and section titles |
| Heading H1        | Semi-bold or bold              | Page-level titles                  |
| Heading H2–H3     | Medium weight                  | Section sub-headings               |
| Body              | Regular weight, comfortable leading | Prose and article content    |
| Caption / Meta    | Small, muted (Zinc 400)        | Dates, labels, metadata            |
| Code              | Monospace                      | Inline code snippets and code blocks |

### Typography Rules
- Headlines should feel confident and direct — short, declarative phrasing is preferred.
- Body copy should be readable at standard widths (~65–75 characters per line).
- Code samples should always use a monospaced font and be visually distinct from prose.
- Avoid using more than two type weights per layout context.

## 6. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
Amware's voice is **direct, intelligent, and human**. It's the voice of someone who has been in the trenches — writing code at scale, leading teams, pitching to investors — and has earned the right to speak plainly about complex things.

### Tone Attributes
- **Confident, not arrogant** — Expertise is demonstrated through clarity, not jargon.
- **Technical, not exclusive** — Depth of knowledge is shared openly, not gatekept.
- **Curious, not performative** — Genuine interest in ideas, tools, and craft comes through naturally.
- **Warm, not casual** — Approachable and human, but always professional in its precision.

### Writing Principles
1. **Say it once, say it well.** Avoid repetition or filler. Every sentence should earn its place.
2. **Lead with the point.** State the main idea first, then support it.
3. **Use active voice.** "I built" not "it was built."
4. **Be specific.** Names, numbers, and examples beat vague generalities every time.
5. **Don't over-explain.** Trust the reader's intelligence.

### Voice Examples

| Avoid                                                      | Prefer                                                   |
|------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| "Leveraging synergistic solutions to optimize outcomes"    | "Building systems that actually work."                   |
| "I am passionate about technology"                         | "I've been taking things apart since I was 8."           |
| "Please feel free to reach out at your earliest convenience" | "Shoot me an email."                                   |
| "Cutting-edge, innovative, best-in-class"                  | "Fast, clean, and built to last."                        |

## 7. Imagery & Visual Style

### Photography
- Amware's visual identity includes **amateur photography** as a personal creative outlet and brand extension.
- Photos should feel authentic, not stock. Real moments, real environments.
- Color-grading should lean toward muted, slightly desaturated tones that align with the dark palette.
- Avoid overly bright, oversaturated, or cliché stock imagery.

### UI & Design Aesthetic
- Interfaces are **minimal, dark-first, and grid-aligned**.
- Layout preferences: clean white space, clear visual hierarchy, and deliberate use of contrast.
- Card-based UI components with subtle borders (Zinc 700) against dark backgrounds.
- No unnecessary decoration — every visual element serves a purpose.
- Design tools of record: **Figma** (design + prototyping + whiteboarding).

### Iconography
- Use simple, line-based or filled icons consistent in weight and style.
- Icons should be sized to align with the surrounding type baseline.
- Prefer minimal, recognizable symbols — avoid overly ornate or illustrative icon styles.

## 10. Brand Don'ts (At a Glance)

- Do not use the logo on cluttered or low-contrast backgrounds.
- Do not use bright, saturated color palettes that conflict with the dark-first design system.
- Do not write in passive voice or use corporate buzzwords.
- Do not use stock imagery that feels generic or inauthentic.
- Do not mix multiple accent colors together — pick one per context.
- Do not represent Amware with aggressive, hype-driven, or clickbait-style copy.
- Do not scale the logo below minimum sizes or alter its proportions.
